# Further Reading & Sources

This book is built on primary standards and high-quality professional and academic sources. The list
below is curated for readers who want to go deeper, grouped by theme. Where a source underpins a
specific chapter, that is noted. Authoritative primary standards are listed first in each group —
always prefer the current official text over any secondary summary.

## The standards (primary — read these first)

- **IAS 7 — *Statement of Cash Flows*** (IFRS Foundation). The governing IFRS standard. — <https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-7-statement-of-cash-flows/>
- **ASC 230 — *Statement of Cash Flows*** (FASB Accounting Standards Codification). The governing US GAAP topic. — <https://asc.fasb.org/>
- **Deloitte IAS Plus — IAS 7 summary.** A clear, maintained synopsis with recent amendments. — <https://www.iasplus.com/en/standards/ias/ias7>
- **KPMG / EY / PwC handbooks on the statement of cash flows.** Big-4 application guidance with worked classification cases. *(Chapter 4)*

## Fundamentals, preparation & analysis

- **CFA Institute — Financial Statement Analysis curriculum**, "Understanding Cash Flow Statements." The analytical backbone for Chapters 1–3. — <https://www.cfainstitute.org/>
- **Corporate Finance Institute (CFI)** — Cash Flow Statement, FCF, FCFF vs FCFE, and working-capital guides. Pedagogically excellent. — <https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/>
- **Wall Street Prep** — cash flow, working-capital cycle, and 13-week-model knowledge base. *(Chapters 2, 3, 6)* — <https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/>
- **AccountingTools** — direct vs. indirect method and line-by-line preparation references. *(Chapters 1–2)*
- **AnalystPrep** — CFA-level notes on cash-flow quality and ratios. *(Chapter 3)*

## Earnings quality, red flags & forensic accounting

- **Sloan, R. (1996), "Do Stock Prices Fully Reflect Information in Accruals and Cash Flows About Future Earnings?"** The foundational accruals-quality paper. — via <https://papers.ssrn.com/>
- **Beneish, M. (1999), "The Detection of Earnings Manipulation."** The M-score model. *(Chapter 5)*
- **Dickinson, V. (2011), "Cash Flow Patterns as a Proxy for Firm Life Cycle."** The canonical 8-pattern / life-cycle mapping used in Chapter 3. — via <https://papers.ssrn.com/>
- **U.S. SEC — Accounting and Auditing Enforcement Releases (AAERs)** and litigation releases (Enron, WorldCom, Dynegy "Project Alpha," Lucent). Primary-source detail on real manipulation. *(Chapter 5)* — <https://www.sec.gov/divisions/enforce/friactions.shtml>
- **Howard Schilit, *Financial Shenanigans*.** The classic practitioner text on detecting reporting tricks, including cash-flow classification games. *(Chapter 5)*

## Forecasting, liquidity & treasury

- **Association for Financial Professionals (AFP)** — liquidity and cash-forecasting surveys and practice guides. *(Chapter 6)*
- **Financial Edge / Wall Street Prep — 13-Week Cash Flow (TWCF) model guides.** Restructuring-grade liquidity forecasting. *(Chapter 6)*
- **Investment-banking three-statement modeling references** — working-capital drivers (DSO/DIO/DPO) wired into the cash flow statement. *(Chapter 6)*

## Valuation

- **Aswath Damodaran (NYU Stern)** — DCF, FCFF/FCFE, cost of capital, and terminal-value materials; his data and spreadsheets are freely available. The reference for Chapter 7. — <https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/>
- **CFA Institute — Equity Valuation: free-cash-flow valuation.** FCFF/FCFE discounting and the enterprise→equity bridge. *(Chapter 7)*
- **McKinsey & Company, *Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies*.** The institutional standard on cash-flow-based valuation.

## Practical investor analysis (Chapters 8–9)

- **Beginner investor-education course materials** — the green-flag / red-flag reading framework and the "which multiple, when" approach in Chapters 8–9 are adapted from a beginner stock-analysis course (cheat sheets and quizzes), reproduced for teaching and cross-referenced with the authoritative sources below.
- **Corporate Finance Institute — valuation multiples.** Clear definitions and use-cases for [P/E](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/price-earnings-ratio/), [P/FCF](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/price-to-free-cash-flow/), [P/OCF](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/price-to-cash-flow-ratio/), and [EV/EBITDA](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/ev-ebitda/). *(Chapter 9)*
- **Corporate Finance Institute — bank valuation.** Why financials are valued on earnings and book value (P/E, P/TBV), not cash-flow multiples. — <https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/how-bank-valuation-works> *(Chapters 6 & 9)*
- **Company data — [stockanalysis.com](https://stockanalysis.com).** The source used to verify the FY2024–2025 company figures (operating cash flow, CapEx, free cash flow, buybacks, dividends) cited in Chapter 8. Always confirm against each company's latest annual report / 10-K.
