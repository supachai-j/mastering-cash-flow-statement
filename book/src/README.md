> **Profit is an opinion; cash is a fact.** A company can report record earnings and still go bankrupt — because earnings are an accounting construct, but payroll, suppliers, and lenders are paid in cash. The statement of cash flows is the one financial statement that is hardest to fake and closest to economic reality. This book teaches you to master it.

## What this book covers

This is a practitioner's guide, built from primary standards (IAS 7, ASC 230), CFA-level analytical frameworks, and real corporate cases. It takes you from "what are the three sections?" to "value a company from its free cash flow and spot when the cash numbers are being dressed up."

| # | Chapter | You will be able to… |
|---|---------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Fundamentals & Structure** | Map any cash flow statement into Operating / Investing / Financing and explain direct vs. indirect |
| 2 | **Preparing the Statement** | Build a cash flow statement from an income statement and two balance sheets |
| 3 | **Analysis & Interpretation** | Compute free cash flow and cash-flow ratios, and read cash-flow patterns |
| 4 | **IFRS vs US GAAP** | Classify interest, dividends, and taxes correctly under each framework |
| 5 | **Red Flags & Forensics** | Detect inflated operating cash flow and classification games |
| 6 | **Application & Forecasting** | Read cash flow across industries and build a 13-week and long-range forecast |
| 7 | **Cash Flow & Valuation** | Run a DCF — FCFF/FCFE, WACC, terminal value — end to end |

A **Formula Sheet**, **Glossary**, and **Further Reading** close the book.

## Who it is for

Finance students, analysts, accountants, founders, and investors who want to stop treating the cash flow statement as the "third statement nobody reads" and start using it as their sharpest analytical tool.
