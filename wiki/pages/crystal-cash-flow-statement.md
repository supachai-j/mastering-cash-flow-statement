## Fundamentals & structure
- The CFS reconciles accrual profit to actual cash; its ending cash must equal balance-sheet cash — the articulation test. [src: raw/01] (conf: 0.95)
- Three mandatory sections: Operating, Investing, Financing (IAS 7.6; ASC 230). [src: raw/01] (conf: 0.97)
- Cash equivalents = readily convertible + insignificant value risk + ≤3 months from *acquisition* date (all three). [src: raw/01] (conf: 0.9)
- Indirect method dominates (>95% of filers) because its inputs come from the accrual ledger; analysts/CFA Institute prefer direct for transparency. [src: raw/01, raw/02] (conf: 0.85)

## Preparation
- Indirect recipe: net income + non-cash (D&A, impairment, SBC, Δ deferred tax) − gains + losses − ΔNWC. [src: raw/02] (conf: 0.95)
- Working-capital sign rule: asset ↑ = cash outflow; liability ↑ = cash inflow. [src: raw/02] (conf: 0.97)

## Analysis
- FCF = OCF − CapEx; FCFF = EBIT×(1−t) + D&A − CapEx − ΔNWC; FCFE = FCFF − Int×(1−t) + net borrowing. [src: raw/03] (conf: 0.95)
- Earnings quality: persistent OCF < net income (high accruals) is a warning; Sloan accruals & cash-realization ratios formalize it. [src: raw/03, raw/05] (conf: 0.85)
- Dickinson (2011): the 8 sign-combinations of (O/I/F) proxy the firm life cycle. [src: raw/03] (conf: 0.8)

## Standards (IFRS vs US GAAP)
- IAS 7 allows policy choice for interest/dividends (Operating/Investing/Financing); ASC 230 largely fixes interest & dividends-received in Operating, dividends-paid in Financing. [src: raw/04] (conf: 0.9)
- US GAAP updates: ASU 2016-15 (classification), 2016-18 (restricted cash); supplier-finance disclosures via IAS 7 amendment (2024) and ASU 2022-04. [src: raw/04] (conf: 0.85)

## Red flags & forensics
- OCF is inflatable via stretching payables, receivables factoring, capitalizing opex, and classification-shifting between sections. [src: raw/05] (conf: 0.85)
- Documented cases: Enron (prepay/Mahonia), WorldCom (line-cost capitalization), Dynegy "Project Alpha," Tesco 2014. [src: raw/05] (conf: 0.9)

## Forecasting & application
- Industry lens: capital-intensive → FCF (OCF−CapEx) is the North Star; asset-light/SaaS → OCF conversion + deferred revenue; retail → negative cash-conversion cycle; banks → a different framework (capital adequacy, NIM). [src: raw/06] (conf: 0.85)
- 13-week direct (TWCF) forecast = tactical liquidity; indirect 3-statement model = strategic; WC drivers are DSO/DIO/DPO, CCC = DSO+DIO−DPO. [src: raw/06] (conf: 0.9)

## Valuation
- Pair the cash flow with its rate: FCFF↔WACC→enterprise value; FCFE↔cost of equity→equity value. Mixing them is the classic DCF error. [src: raw/07] (conf: 0.95)
- Terminal value (Gordon): TV = FCF_N×(1+g)/(WACC−g); often the majority of DCF value, hence sensitivity-test g and WACC. [src: raw/07] (conf: 0.9)
