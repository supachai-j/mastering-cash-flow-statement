# Ch3 sources — Analysis & interpretation

Retrieved: 2026-06-16

---

- [Free Cash Flow Valuation — CFA Institute Refresher Reading 2026](https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/professional-learning/refresher-readings/2026/free-cash-flow-valuation) — Authoritative primary source for all FCFF and FCFE formulas and valuation mechanics; CFA curriculum level.
  - "FCFF = NI + NCC + Int(1 – Tax rate) – FCInv – WCInv"
  - "FCFF = EBIT(1 – Tax rate) + Dep – FCInv – WCInv"
  - "FCFF = EBITDA(1 – Tax rate) + Dep(Tax rate) – FCInv – WCInv"
  - "FCFF = CFO + Int(1 – Tax rate) – FCInv"
  - "FCFE = NI + NCC – FCInv – WCInv + Net borrowing" and "FCFE = CFO – FCInv + Net borrowing"
  - "FCFE = FCFF – Int(1 – Tax rate) + Net borrowing" (bridge formula)
  - Analysts prefer FCF methods when companies don't pay dividends or pay dividends inconsistent with capacity

- [Quality of Earnings Ratio — Wall Street Prep](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/quality-of-earnings-ratio/) — Covers QoE formula, benchmarks, and warning signs.
  - "QoE = Cash from Operations ÷ Net Income"
  - "QoE > 1.0x → Higher Quality Income — earnings are reliable, not distorted by manipulation"
  - "QoE < 1.0x → Lower Quality Income — cash operations lag reported earnings, potential red flag"
  - "Not all earnings are equal, i.e. earnings are not equal to cash flow"
  - Rising accounts receivable relative to revenue signals revenue recognition risk
  - "CFO = Net Income + D&A – Increase in NWC"

- [Cash Flow Ratios — AnalystNotes CFA Level 1](https://analystnotes.com/cfa-study-notes-cash-flow-ratios-and-common-size-analysis.html) — Comprehensive CFA-aligned ratio set with formulas and interpretations.
  - Cash Flow Per Share = (CFO – Preferred dividends) / Common shares outstanding
  - Reinvestment Ratio = CFO / Cash paid for long-term assets
  - Debt Coverage = CFO / Total debt; Interest Coverage = (CFO + Interest Paid + Taxes Paid) / Interest Paid
  - Dividend Payment = CFO / Dividends paid
  - Cash Return on Assets = CFO / Average total assets; Cash Return on Equity = CFO / Average shareholders' equity

- [Cash Flow Ratios — AccountingTools](https://www.accountingtools.com/articles/cash-flow-ratios.html) — Practical definitions and ratio interpretations for practitioners.
  - Cash Flow Margin = CFO / Net Sales ("a clear picture of the amount of cash generated per dollar of sales")
  - "If [current liability coverage] is less than 1:1, a business is not generating enough cash to pay for its immediate obligations"
  - Price-to-Cash-Flow "is qualitatively better than the price/earnings ratio, since it uses cash flows instead of reported earnings"

- [Cash Reinvestment Ratio — AccountingTools](https://www.accountingtools.com/articles/cash-reinvestment-ratio) — Formula and caveats for the reinvestment coverage metric.
  - Formula: (Increase in fixed assets + Increase in WC) / (NI + Non-cash expenses – Non-cash sales – Dividends)
  - Appropriateness is industry-dependent; 51% presented as normal for a rapidly-expanding business
  - Compare fixed-asset-to-revenue and WC-to-revenue against industry peers; outperformance at lower ratio = efficiency

- [Cash Flow Quality: The Ultimate Guide — Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/academy/analysis/cash-flow-quality-guide/) — Practitioner guide on OCF vs NI, quality signals, and adequacy ratio.
  - "Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, but cash is reality"
  - Cash Flow Adequacy Ratio = OCF / (CapEx + Debt Repayments + Dividends); >1.0 = self-sufficient
  - CapEx-to-OCF = CapEx / OCF; high ratio = growth phase or capital inefficiency
  - Persistently negative FCF in mature companies = warning sign
  - Cash growth driven by financing (not operations) = structural concern

- [Free Cash Flow Yield — Wall Street Prep](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/free-cash-flow-yield/) — Unlevered vs levered FCF yield formulas and interpretation.
  - Unlevered FCF Yield = FCFF / Enterprise Value
  - Levered FCF Yield = FCFE / Equity Value = FCF per Share / Share Price
  - "More practical and broadly used in certain industries, like private equity" vs. dividend yield
  - Example: unlevered FCF yield 9.2% vs levered 5.1% — gap reflects debt service cost

- [Earnings Quality — Financial Edge Training](https://www.fe.training/free-resources/accounting/earnings-quality/) — Accruals framework including cash conversion ratio and Sloan-style accruals ratio.
  - Cash Conversion Ratio = OCF / EBITDA
  - Accruals Ratio = (NI – OCF – CFI) / Average(Total Assets – Cash)
  - "Low cash conversion ratio and high accruals ratio = indicator of low earnings quality"
  - "Working capital demand higher than expected" despite company size = warning sign

- [FCFE from Net Income — Corporate Finance Institute](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/how-to-calculate-fcfe-from-net-income) — FCFE derivation and FCFF-to-FCFE bridge explanation.
  - FCFF = NI + D&A – CapEx – ΔWC + Int(1 – t)
  - FCFE = FCFF + Net Borrowing – Int(1 – t); alternatively FCFE = NI + D&A – CapEx – ΔWC + Net Borrowing
  - FCFF discounted at WACC → firm value; subtract debt → equity value
  - FCFE discounted at cost of equity → equity value directly

- [Dickinson, V. (2011). Cash Flow Patterns as a Proxy for Firm Life Cycle. *The Accounting Review*, 86, 1964–1994.](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=755804) — Canonical academic source for 5-stage / 8-combination life cycle classification.
  - Five stages defined purely by CFO/CFI/CFF sign combinations (no subjective variables)
  - Introduction: CFO−, CFI−, CFF+ / Growth: CFO+, CFI−, CFF+ / Maturity: CFO+, CFI−, CFF−
  - Decline: CFO−, CFI+, CFF− / Shakeout: four residual combinations (transitional)
  - "Captures the nonlinear relation of firm life cycle with firm profitability, size, and age"
  - Framework is parsimonious and requires only the cash flow statement to classify
