# Ch4 sources — IFRS (IAS 7) vs US GAAP (ASC 230)

Retrieved: 2026-06-16

---

## Sources

- **KPMG US — "Statement of cash flows: IFRS® Accounting Standards vs US GAAP" (2023)**
  URL: https://kpmg.com/us/en/articles/2023/ifrs-accounting-standards-us-gaap1.html
  - Interest paid: IFRS "operating or financing"; US GAAP "generally operating" (excluding capitalized interest)
  - Dividends paid: IFRS "operating or financing"; US GAAP financing activities only
  - Interest/dividends received: IFRS "operating or investing"; US GAAP operating only
  - Taxes paid: IFRS "generally operating unless identifiable with investing/financing"; US GAAP always operating
  - Bank overdrafts: IFRS may reduce cash balance if "repayable on demand and integral to cash management"; US GAAP financing liabilities only
  - Restricted cash: IFRS excluded if restrictions fail purpose test; US GAAP included in total cash reconciliation with separate balance sheet presentation (post-ASU 2016-18)
  - Indirect method starting point: IFRS allows multiple (pre-tax profit, post-tax, operating profit); US GAAP requires net income

- **Deloitte DART — "Appendix E: Differences Between U.S. GAAP and IFRS" (ASC 230 Roadmap)**
  URL: https://dart.deloitte.com/USDART/home/codification/presentation/asc230-10/roadmap-statement-cash-flow/appendix-e-differences-between-us-gaap/appendix-e-differences-between-us-gaap
  - US GAAP cannot include bank overdrafts within cash equivalents; IFRS may if part of cash management
  - US GAAP: restricted cash must be included in beginning/ending cash balances regardless of balance sheet presentation
  - US GAAP: detailed prescriptive guidance per category; IFRS: flexible, judgment-based, nature-of-activity principle
  - US GAAP: reconciliation required under both direct and indirect methods; IFRS: reconciliation only mandatory if indirect method chosen (direct method users must show indirect reconciliation in notes — IAS 7.67A)
  - Scope: US GAAP exempts certain pension plans and investment companies; IAS 7: all entities must present

- **IFRS.org — "IASB increases transparency of companies' supplier finance" (May 2023)**
  URL: https://www.ifrs.org/news-and-events/news/2023/05/iasb-increases-transparency-of-companies-supplier-finance/
  - Amendments to IAS 7 and IFRS 7 address "insufficient visibility of supplier finance arrangements"
  - Effective for annual reporting periods beginning on or after 1 January 2024
  - Requires: terms and conditions; carrying amounts of financial liabilities in arrangements; amounts already paid by finance providers to suppliers
  - Requires: ranges of payment due dates compared with non-program comparable payables
  - Non-cash changes in carrying amounts of financial liabilities must also be disclosed

- **EY Global — "IASB amendments to IAS 7 and IFRS 7 for supplier finance arrangements"**
  URL: https://www.ey.com/en_gl/technical/ifrs-technical-resources/iasb-amendments-to-ias-7-and-ifrs-7-for-supplier-finance-arrangements
  - Disclosure of "effects of supplier finance arrangements on their liabilities, cash flows and exposure to liquidity risk" required
  - Effective 1 January 2024; early adoption permitted; no comparative year required in first application year
  - Does NOT change classification or presentation — disclosure only amendment

- **Deloitte DART — "Heads Up: FASB Issues ASU 2022-04 — Supplier Finance Programs" (September 2022)**
  URL: https://dart.deloitte.com/USDART/home/publications/deloitte/heads-up/2022/fasb-asu-supplier-finance-programs
  - Requires disclosure of key terms including "payment terms and assets pledged as security"
  - Confirmed amounts at period end, balance sheet location, and rollforward (beginning/additions/settled/ending) required annually
  - Interim requirement: confirmed outstanding amounts each interim period
  - Effective FY beginning after 15 Dec 2022 (general); rollforward after 15 Dec 2023

- **RSM US — "Presentation of restricted cash in the statement of cash flows" (ASU 2016-18)**
  URL: https://rsmus.com/insights/financial-reporting/presentation-of-restricted-cash-in-the-statement-of-cash-flows.html
  - ASU 2016-18: requires explanation of change in "total of cash, cash equivalents and amounts generally described as restricted cash"
  - Restricted cash included with cash when reconciling opening/closing balances
  - Transfers between cash and restricted cash no longer reported as operating/investing/financing activities
  - Effective: public entities fiscal years beginning after 15 Dec 2017; others Dec 2018; retrospective application

- **KPMG US — "IFRS 18 changes the statement of cash flows" (2026)**
  URL: https://kpmg.com/us/en/articles/2026/ifrs-18-changes-statement-cash-flows.html
  - IFRS 18 amends IAS 7: non-SMBA entities — interest paid → Financing; interest received → Investing; dividends paid → Financing; dividends received → Investing
  - SMBA (financial institution) entities: classify based on how related income/expense appears in income statement
  - Requires "operating profit or loss" as starting point for indirect method; removes current multiple-starting-point flexibility
  - Effective annual and interim periods beginning on or after 1 January 2027; comparative periods restated

- **McClintock &amp; Associates — "Statement of Cash Flows Update" (ASU 2016-15 summary)**
  URL: https://mcclintockcpa.com/statement-cash-flows-update/
  - ASU 2016-15 eight items: (1) debt prepayment costs → Financing; (2) zero-coupon bond settlements → bifurcated interest to Operating, principal to Financing; (3) contingent consideration → ≤3 months: Investing, >3 months: Financing, excess: Operating; (4) insurance proceeds → nature-based (inventory: Operating; PP&amp;E: Investing); (5) COLI proceeds → Investing; (6) equity method distributions → policy choice; (7) beneficial interest in securitization → Investing; (8) mixed flows → predominance principle
  - Effective FY beginning after 15 Dec 2017 (public); 15 Dec 2018 (others)

- **Chartered Accountants Worldwide — "IAS 7 Statement of Cash Flows and Its Proposed Amendments"**
  URL: https://charteredaccountantsworldwide.com/ias-7-statement-of-cash-flows-and-its-proposed-amendments/
  - IAS 7.18: entities may report operating cash flows using direct or indirect method
  - IAS 7.19: "entities are encouraged to report cash flows from operating activities using the direct method"
  - IAS 7.31–33: interest/dividends received and paid "presented separately and consistently" each period
  - IAS 7.34: dividends paid "may be classified as a financing cash flow because they are a cost of obtaining financial resources"

- **BDO Australia — "Cash and Cash Equivalents" (IAS 7 commentary)**
  URL: https://www.bdo.com.au/en-au/blogs/corporate-reporting/cash-and-cash-equivalents
  - IAS 7: three months from acquisition date (not original maturity) — critical difference from US GAAP
  - Bank overdrafts "repayable on demand and form an integral part of an entity's cash management processes are included as cash equivalents"
  - ASC 230: "original maturity to the entity" — a 10-year bond purchased 2 months before maturity does NOT qualify under US GAAP
  - Cash equivalents classification assigned at initial recognition remains unchanged as investment approaches maturity
